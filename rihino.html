About the Rhinoceros

Appearance:

Rhinos are large, heavy mammals with thick, gray or brown skin, short legs, and one or two horns on their nose. The word "rhinoceros" comes from the Greek words rhin- or rhis, which means "nose," and keras, which means "horn".

Size:

Rhinos can weigh over 3 tonnes, with white rhinos being the largest species and Sumatran rhinos being the smallest.

Diet:

Rhinos are herbivores that eat plants.

Endangered status:

Rhinos are endangered due to poaching.

Importance:

Rhinos are important to the ecosystems they live in, and to the many other species that share their habitat, including elephants, buffalo, and large carnivores.

Scientific Name:

Rhinocerotidae

Habitat:

tropical and subtropical grasslands, savannas and shrublands, tropical moist forests, deserts and shrublands are among their habitats.

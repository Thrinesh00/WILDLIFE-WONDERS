About the Hippopotamus

Size

Hippos are one of the largest mammals on Earth, weighing up to 8,000 pounds and reaching lengths of 16.5 feet. They are comparable in size to white and Indian rhinoceroses.

Appearance

Hippos have rotund bodies and their eyes, ears, and nose are located on top of their heads.

Diet

Hippos are herbivores that eat short grasses and fallen fruit. They can eat up to 150 pounds of grass per night.
